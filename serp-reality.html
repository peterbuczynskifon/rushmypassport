SERP Analysis

What's Actually Winning in 2026 US Passport SERPs

Four structural forces are reshaping every commercial passport query. Understanding them is a prerequisite for every ranking decision below.

A
AI Overview synthesis
High-volume informational queries now trigger AI Overviews pulling from 5–8 authoritative sources — almost always travel.state.gov, one major outlet, plus 2–3 of USPS, AAA, Forbes, NerdWallet. Recency markers like "2026" are explicitly weighted.
B
Local Pack + State Dept dominance
For "passport agency near me" and similar, the SERP is owned by Google Maps Map Pack and travel.state.gov's official locator. Private expediter pages compete for positions 4–8 under a very fat zero-click zone — explaining the 76–91% click collapse.
C
Ultra-specific city blog posts
11 city blog posts delivered 8,242 clicks vs. 21 /passport-office/[city]/ pages delivering 2,271. Google rewards narrative, problem-first format over brittle "here are our services" templates — by a factor of ~4x.
D
Registered authority + transparency
Commercial SERPs now heavily favor pages surfacing State Department registration, hand-carry credentials, explicit turnaround guarantees, pricing transparency, and reviewer identity. Thin affiliate content is increasingly filtered.
